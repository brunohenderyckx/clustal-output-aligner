Step 1 – Design input sequences for Clustal Omega tool

We retrieved amino acid sequences from the NCBI – protein database (https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/protein).



Press “search” to list La protein in the organism Homo sapiens. This will reveal a list of potential matches.



Press “FASTA” to directly link to the amino acid sequence in fasta format compatible with Clustal Omega. All sequences are obtained in fasta format which is a text-based format where the first line contains a “>” followed by a unique identifier and the second line contains the protein, RNA or DNA sequence. Step 2 – Enter list of fasta sequences in Clustal Omega


Click the link to go to the Clustal Omega website for alignments: https://www.ebi.ac.uk/Tools/msa/clustalo/



Enter your fasta sequences of interest in the Clustal Omega tool:



Click “More options…” to ensure that all sequences are compared to Homo sapiens by changing “ORDER” from “aligned” to “input”





Step 3 – Enter the alignments from Clustal Omega in the Alignment Visualization tool

Copy the alignment results and paste them in the Alignment Visualization tool.



Custom changes to the alignment matches can be made by typing in your conserved amino acid matching residues of interest in the “Advanced Setting” box.



The standard used matching of conserved amino acids are:
  • DENQ
  • KRH
  • FWY
  • VILM
  • ST
No conservation comparison is done for following amino acids:
  • C
  • G
  • P
  • A
